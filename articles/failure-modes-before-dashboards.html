Why predictive maintenance starts with failure modes, not dashboards

Before choosing analytics, understand what actually breaks, how it degrades, and which measurement can see it early enough to matter.

The common mistake

Many projects begin with dashboards and platform demos. The more useful starting point is simpler: what asset hurts production when it fails, what component degrades, and what evidence appears before the failure becomes obvious?

The technical reality

A bearing, gearbox, pump, fan, conveyor or hydraulic system does not fail because a dashboard says so. It fails through physical mechanisms. The sensor strategy should be chosen around those mechanisms.

The practical answer

Start with asset criticality, failure mode, operating context and existing data. Then decide whether vibration, temperature, current, pressure, flow, speed or process data is the right signal.

Workshop questions

What fails most often? What causes the biggest stoppage? What early warning signs have technicians noticed? Which signals already exist? Where would a recommendation need to appear for people to act?
