# Content Plan

## Core positioning

**One-line brand statement**

I help manufacturers bridge the gap between mechanical machinery, sensor data, automation architecture and scalable predictive maintenance value.

## Website content buckets

### 1. Tom Talks Predictive Maintenance

Short 60-second videos for LinkedIn and the website.

Starter topics:

1. Why predictive maintenance starts with failure modes, not dashboards
2. The difference between condition monitoring and predictive maintenance
3. What makes a machine worth monitoring?
4. Why vibration is powerful, but not always the answer
5. The role of temperature, current, flow and pressure in asset health
6. Why pilots fail to scale
7. How to think about IT/OT architecture without drowning in jargon
8. Why maintenance teams do not need more alarms
9. What a good PdM business case looks like
10. How to choose the first assets for a PdM project

### 2. Technical articles

Longer written posts to build authority.

Suggested article structure:

- The customer problem
- The common mistake
- The technical reality
- The practical answer
- What I would ask in a workshop

### 3. Field notes

Short posts/photos from events, workshops, diagrams, machine examples and architecture sketches.

Examples:

- "What this pump tells us about sensor choice"
- "Why the historian was not the whole answer"
- "A simple way to explain edge vs cloud"
- "Three questions I ask before recommending a sensor"

### 4. Case-study style posts

Only use anonymised or publicly approved information.

Safe format:

- Industry context
- Asset type
- Problem pattern
- Technical challenge
- Learning
- Generalised takeaway

## Tone

Professional, calm, technically credible, commercially aware.

Avoid:

- Overclaiming
- Confidential customer details
- Corporate cliché
- "AI magic" language
- Sounding like a generic software salesperson

Use:

- Specific machinery examples
- Practical engineering judgement
- Clear commercial value
- Simple diagrams and analogies
